# Notso.ai Visual Style Lock — v1.0

> This document locks the visual identity for every mascot image generated by
> the notso-proposal-gen system. Every Gemini / nano-banana / Replicate image
> call MUST go through `notso_style_prompt.build_style_prompt()` so that the
> rules below are enforced at the prompt layer. Locked on 2026-04-12.

---

## 1. Core material
Matte clay / foam / vinyl designer-toy finish. Smooth even surface, smoother
than stop-motion clay (think vinyl collectible rather than Plasticine). No
gloss, no plastic shine, no metallic reflection, no holographic accents.

Inspirations to pull from: Funko Pop silhouettes, Pop Mart blind-box proportions,
designer vinyl toy finishes, Pixar-level micro-expressiveness. Inspirations to
stay away from: Disney Zootopia fur simulation, stylized anime, cel-shaded
comic looks, Y2K / cyberpunk aesthetics.

The phrase "distinctive Notso AI signature" must appear in every prompt so the
model does not simply clone an existing toy brand.

---

## 2. Proportion system (two tiers, never mix within one character)

**Hero Tier — adult scale, head-to-body ~1:2.**
Defined shoulders, posture, hands that can hold objects and perform precise
gestures. Reference characters: Fin, Willy, Biek. Use this tier for main brand
mascot, hero shots, slide covers, product interaction scenes, the "living 3D
character" hero framing.

**Chibi Tier — toy scale, head-to-body ~1:1.3 to 1:1.5.**
Oversized head, short stubby limbs, Funko-Pop / designer vinyl collectible
silhouette. Reference characters: Woof, Jumbo supporting lineup. Use this tier
for family lineups, blind-box renders, supporting characters, and slide 16
merchandising visuals.

One character locks to one tier for its whole lifecycle — never mix Hero and
Chibi proportions for the same mascot identity.

---

## 3. Eyes — locked formula
- Large round eyes, single solid black pupil
- One small white highlight point, always in the same relative position
- No iris detail, no gradient, no reflective catchlight shapes
- No eyelash rendering (upper lash is implied by eyelid shape only)

Emotional variation comes from eyebrows, mouth shape, and head tilt. Eye detail
does NOT vary per expression. This is the strongest visual anchor of the Notso
family — if a generated image fails this rule, it is rejected.

---

## 4. Eyebrows — primary emotion carrier
Thick, solid, dark, and independent from hair colour. Can be near-black even
on light-haired characters. Must read clearly from a distance. Because eyes
are simplified, roughly 80% of emotion is carried by eyebrow angle and mouth
curve — so eyebrows must never be thin, faded, or blended into hair.

---

## 5. Lighting
Soft three-point studio lighting:
- Key light from upper camera-left, warm temperature
- Fill light from camera-right, low intensity
- Rim light: one thin bright edge on the silhouette

No harsh cast shadows, no dramatic contrast, no environment colour bounce.
Ground contact shadow is a soft subtle halo only (and because we use
transparent PNG output, it is usually omitted entirely).

**Moody variant** (for pain-point slides like slide 3 or slide 8 negative
emotions): near-black backdrop, single low-angle warm light, rim light stays,
contemplative posture. Reference: Willy on office chair with stressed
expression.

---

## 6. Background — ALWAYS transparent PNG (critical rule)
Every exported mascot asset has a real alpha channel. The background is
provided by the slide template layer at rendering time, not baked into the
image. This is non-negotiable.

- Post-processing pipeline runs `rembg` on every output
- Feathered alpha edges (1–2 px) to prevent jaggies when scaled
- If the model returns a baked background, it is re-processed automatically
- If rembg cannot cleanly separate the foreground, the image is rejected

Previous drafts of this document proposed "Pastel Card / Cream Grid / Dark
Cinematic" as three background modes — that design is **deprecated**.
All exports are transparent PNG, period.

---

## 7. Colour — one signature per character

Brand colour goes on: vest, hoodie, cap, name tag, scarf, apron, tie, glasses
frame, shoes, accessories.

Brand colour does NOT go on: skin, fur, hair, eye colour, eye highlight.

Skin / fur stays neutral (warm beige, light brown, white, grey, tan, peach).
Each character has one dominant brand colour plus at most one accent. Even
when the client's brand is strongly associated with one colour (e.g. a fully
red client), the character's body stays neutral — the brand colour lives on
the wardrobe only. This rule has no exceptions.

---

## 8. Species options
Four levels on the personification spectrum.

**Level 1 — Humanoid.** Stylized person. Safest, best emotional range, best
for main brand mascot. Reference: Fin, Willy, Liza, Biek.

**Level 2 — Personified animal.** Real or recognizable animal with humanlike
posture, clothing, and emotion. Retains species features (ears, tail, fur
pattern) but acts human. Reference: Woof the husky.

**Level 3 — Personified object.** An everyday object brought to life with a
simple face, small hands, and small feet, keeping its base shape and colour.
Examples: a walking chili pepper, a dumpling with eyes, a QR code with arms,
a coffee cup wearing a hat.

**Level 4 — Pure abstract form. DISALLOWED.** No fixed body outline (talking
clouds, moving blobs, geometric spirits, bolts of light, etc.) are NOT
supported. Rationale: per-image consistency is too low for a family-style
lock, and Notso characters must all read as members of the same family.
Requests for Level 4 are redirected to Level 3 (personified object) —
for example "a bolt of light" becomes "a lightbulb with a face, arms, and
legs".

Levels 1–3 all obey: matte clay texture, locked eye formula, rim lighting,
transparent background, neutral base colour with brand accents.

---

## 9. Prohibited content (global negatives)
Applied as negative keywords on every generation call.

- No firearms, no weapons
- No religious symbols (crosses, crescents, Stars of David, etc.)
- No nudity, no revealing clothing
- No political symbols, no flags used as political statement
- No smoking, no alcohol unless explicitly brand-required
- No hard black outlines
- No anime style, no manga, no cel-shaded comic look
- No Y2K / cyberpunk / neon / holographic aesthetics
- No metallic finishes, no chrome, no glass refraction
- No motion blur, no speed lines
- No realistic hair strand simulation
- No photorealistic skin pores
- No brand logos on skin or face
- No text on face
- No baked backgrounds (including cream grids, pastel cards, studio backdrops)
- No drop shadow baked into the image

---

## 10. Identity rules (carried over from the Notso AI brand PDF draft)
- The mascot must not self-identify as an AI or a robot in any dialogue or
  speech bubble. Position it as a "digital colleague" or a "friend".
- Do not fabricate product specs, prices, or knowledge-base content in any
  rendered text on or near the mascot.
- Distributor partners cannot rebrand the product. The "Notso AI" name must
  remain visible wherever the mascot appears in a UI context.
- In chat dialogue renders (e.g. slide 10 flowchart), keep speech under 350
  characters or 3–4 short sentences. One message contains at most one
  question and one link.

---
